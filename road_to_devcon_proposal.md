## 1. Project Overview

Cypher Space, the Blockchain Interest Group at NSS College of Engineering, Palakkad, proposes a comprehensive Ethereum Builder Series to take our student community from blockchain enthusiasts to actual builders. Building on our foundation of awareness sessions and theoretical workshops conducted over the past three months, this program will provide hands-on experience with smart contract development and DApp building — exactly the practical skills that the Ethereum ecosystem needs.

This proposal requests $300 USD in funding and 10 complimentary Devcon 8 tickets to support our mission of bringing the next generation of Ethereum builders from Kerala to Mumbai in November 2026.

## 6. Proposed Program: Road to Devcon 8

Our program spans the month of July 2026, structured in three progressive phases that take students from foundational preparation to a capstone hackathon. This window leverages the period when KTU's new semester (S3/S5/S7) is underway but before the first-year induction program begins in August, offering maximum student availability and optimal resource access.

### Phase 1 — Async Preparation (July 1–5)

Format: Fully online | Duration: 5 days | Effort: Low

Rather than a traditional reading group, Phase 1 is a curated async preparation period. Participants work through a curated video playlist covering Ethereum fundamentals, Solidity basics, and development environment setup. Each module includes a short checkpoint quiz to verify understanding.

This phase serves as a low-effort filter — it ensures that students arriving at the in-person workshop already have foundational knowledge, while naturally filtering for those who are genuinely committed to building.


### Phase 2 — Workshop-Bootcamp Hybrid (July 14–16)

Format: In-person, Computer Lab | Duration: 3 full days

Building on the strong demand for both workshops (30 votes) and bootcamps (21 votes) from our community survey, Phase 2 combines both formats into an intensive three-day hands-on program:

Day 1 — Ethereum Fundamentals & Solidity Basics

- Introduction to Ethereum architecture and the EVM
- Solidity syntax: variables, functions, visibility modifiers
- Setting up the development environment (Remix IDE, Hardhat)

Day 2 — Smart Contract Development & Testing

- Writing and deploying a smart contract to Sepolia testnet
- Verifying contracts on Etherscan
- Working with mappings, arrays, and data structures
- Writing basic test cases for contract logic

Day 3 — DApp Integration & Deployment

- Connecting contracts to a frontend (HTML/JavaScript)
- Integrating MetaMask wallet interaction
- Deploying and testing the complete DApp flow
Expected outcome: Each student deploys at least one smart contract to Sepolia testnet and demonstrates a working mini-DApp connected to their MetaMask wallet.


### Phase 3 — Mini Hackathon & Showcase (July 22–23)

Format: In-person (day sessions) + remote (evening work) | Duration: 2 days

Teams of 3–4 students apply everything they've learned to build a blockchain project from scratch. The hackathon runs across two days:

Day 1 — Kickoff & Build

- Team formation and project ideation
- In-person building session during the day
- Participants continue working from home in the evening

Day 2 — Build, Submit & Demo

- Morning: Finalize projects
- Afternoon: Project submission, live demos, and judging
Possible project themes include simple voting contracts, token transfer applications, document timestamp verification, and basic marketplace prototypes. The best projects receive recognition and are documented for the EF report.
